Skillet Pepper and Garlic Pork

Delicious! Very peppery, so you’ve gotta be a fan of pepper and like a kick. The only change I made was to add some cracked salt to both sides of the pork during the browning phase. Garlic lovers may also want to throw another clove or two in.

    Ingredients

  • 4 (1 1/2 inches thick) boneless center-cut pork chops
  • 4 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 tablespoons coarsely ground black pepper


Simple Hamburger Stroganoff

This has a good base to work with, how ever I cut the cream cheese, sour cream and soup in half, I added garlic and onion to the meat and salt and pepper to taste. It turned out great, the cream cheese add a good flavor. My 6yr old loved it, and it was quick and easy. Will use in the future.

    Ingredients

  • 1 (16 ounce) package egg noodles
  • 1 (8 ounce) package cream cheese, cut into pieces
  • 1 (6 ounce) can chopped mushrooms, with liquid
  • 1 (.75 ounce) packet dry brown gravy mix
  • 2 (10.5 ounce) cans condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 (8 ounce) container sour cream
  • ½ cup milk


Chicken Stir-Fry

Store your leftovers in a shallow, airtight container for up to five days. Reheat in the microwave or on the stove until piping hot. If you're worried about the rice drying out, add a splash of water before reheating.

    Ingredients

  • 2 cups white rice
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 3 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil, divided
  • 1 head broccoli, broken into florets
  • 1 onion, cut into large chunks
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 1 (8 ounce) can sliced water chestnuts, drained
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into matchsticks
